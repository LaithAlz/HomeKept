# HomeKept · Marketing Video Production Brief

Generation-ready prompts for the launch video set (AI video — Higgsfield or equivalent).
Companion to `pricing-and-visits.md` (positioning, legal rails) and the v2 design system
(`frontend/src/styles.css`, `mockups/v2/`).

## Hard rules (from the legal rails)

1. **No fabricated social proof.** No AI-generated "customers," no testimonials, no
   invented stats or review counts. AI video is used for atmosphere, concept, and
   product UI — never to imply real households or real people endorsing us.
2. **No claims of licensed-trade work** — never show us opening a furnace or panel.
   Hands shown doing only what the visit calendar scopes to us: filters, gutters, detectors, checklists, photos, caulking, hardware tightening, hose-bib valves, condenser rinse (disconnect pulled), pressure gauges, vent brushing. Never inside a furnace, panel, or on a roof.
3. **AI-content disclosure** where the platform requires it (label on upload).
4. Every video ends on the standard end card. Soft CTA always: the *free walk-through*,
   never the subscription price.

## Brand kit (paste into every generation as the style block)

> **STYLE:** Warm, calm, editorial home-life cinematography. Soft natural morning light,
> gentle film grain. Palette anchored in warm cream (#F8F5EE), deep pine green (#1E3A2B),
> honey amber (#DE8F3F) accents. Canadian suburban homes — brick and siding two-storeys,
> maple trees, four distinct seasons. Slow, steady camera moves (slow push-in, gentle
> pan); no whip cuts, no drone swoops. Shallow depth of field. Domestic textures: wood,
> linen, houseplants, mugs. Mood: exhale, order, quiet competence. No people's faces in
> focus (hands, silhouettes, backs only). Photorealistic.

> **END CARD (final 2.5s, every video):** Cream (#F8F5EE) background, subtle paper
> grain. Centered leaf mark (pine circle with one clipped corner, honey core) above
> "HomeKept" in a warm serif (Fraunces). Below, smaller: "Home maintenance on
> autopilot." Then: "Book a free 90-minute walk-through → homekept.ca". A thin honey
> underline draws in beneath "free."

**Text overlays:** Fraunces (or closest warm serif), pine on cream / cream on pine,
lower-third or centered, generous size — every video must work **with sound off**.
**Masters:** 9:16 (Reels/TikTok/FB/Nextdoor) + 16:9 recrop (site, YouTube).
**VO (optional layer):** warm, unhurried, Canadian-neutral; text overlays carry the
message regardless.

**Series notes:**
- Overlay months/items must match the visit calendar in `pricing-and-visits.md` — these episodes are that table, dramatized.
- **Tier accuracy:** Feb/May/Aug/Dec visits are Premier-only and Mar/Jun/Sep/Nov are Complete+. Captions say "on the plan," never "on *your* plan" — don't imply every tier includes every month.
- Any statistic used in an overlay or caption must have a linkable source verified *before generation*; when in doubt use the consequence framing ("lint is why…") instead of a number.
- **Practical inserts:** the fridge list (V1 s1/s3) and any legible paper are $0 real-prop macro shots — shoot them, don't generate them. AI-rendered text is the tell.
- **Phone/app screens:** generate devices with blank or neutral screens and composite real frontend screenshots (or mockups/v2 captures) in the edit — models will invent a fake UI and break the brand tokens.
- **Hands:** highest AI failure risk. Prefer extreme close-ups mid-motion with minimal finger articulation — or shoot hero hand shots practically (the style block already bans faces, so founders' hands work fine).
- Generate 9:16 and 16:9 as separate renders or frame center-safe — don't crop one from the other.

## Production workflow (when the MCP is connected)

1. Generate scene-by-scene (one prompt per row), 2–3 candidates per scene
2. Founder picks takes; regenerate only failed scenes (consistency: reuse seed/style refs)
3. Assemble + overlay text in the edit (don't ask the model to render typography —
   AI text is the tell); add the end card from a single master built in a design tool from real brand assets (never AI-generated typography or logo)
4. File under `marketing/video/<id>/` with the chosen takes and final masters
5. Track in PostHog/UTM: every link is `homekept.ca/book?utm_source=<channel>&utm_campaign=<video-id>`
